Grievance and Disciplinary Policy - American Express Digital Workplace

1. Purpose
This policy ensures that all employee grievances are handled fairly, consistently, and in a timely manner. It also outlines the disciplinary process for conduct and performance issues.

2. Raising a Grievance
Employees who have a workplace concern should first discuss it informally with their direct manager. If the issue cannot be resolved informally, a formal grievance can be submitted via the HR portal within 30 days of the incident. The HR team will acknowledge the grievance within 3 working days.

3. Grievance Investigation
A designated HR Business Partner will investigate the grievance. The process includes gathering evidence, interviewing relevant parties, and reviewing documentation. The investigation will be completed within 15 working days. Employees will receive a written outcome.

4. Appeals
If an employee is not satisfied with the grievance outcome, they may appeal in writing within 10 working days. The appeal will be reviewed by a Senior HR Leader not previously involved in the case.

5. Disciplinary Process
Disciplinary action may be taken for conduct issues including misconduct, poor attendance, breach of company policy, or performance failures. The stages are:
  - Verbal Warning: For minor or first-time issues. Recorded in the employee's file for 6 months.
  - Written Warning: For repeated or more serious issues. Remains on file for 12 months.
  - Final Written Warning: For serious or persistent issues. Remains on file for 18 months.
  - Dismissal: For gross misconduct or failure to improve after previous warnings.

6. Gross Misconduct
Acts of gross misconduct may result in immediate dismissal without prior warnings. Examples include theft, fraud, physical violence, serious data breaches, harassment, or being under the influence of alcohol or drugs at work.

7. Support During the Process
Employees under investigation or disciplinary proceedings may be accompanied by a colleague or union representative at formal meetings. Confidentiality will be maintained throughout the process. The Employee Assistance Program (EAP) is available for emotional support.
